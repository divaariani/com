Project information

  • Category: Mobile Application
  • Role: Mobile Developer
  • Project date: Jan 2024 - Feb 2024
  • Project URL: github.com/Trace2Treat/Application
  • Description: Trace2Treat is a platform for promoting unsold food products that offers solutions to two serious problems: food waste and plastic waste. The platform offers a sustainable waste management solution by engaging UMKM players as partners, allowing customers to access affordable food and pay with points earned through exchanging plastic waste. Customers can pay foods with point from exchange plastic waste. This plastic waste will processed and recycled by partner collectors.

Problem

The challenge addressed by Trace2Treat is the dual problem of food waste and plastic waste in the food industry. The problem is to mitigate the environmental impact of discarded food products and plastic waste, Trace2Treat seeks to provide a sustainable solution by collaborating with small and medium-sized enterprises (UMKM) in the food industry.

Solution

The challenge involves optimizing the collection and recycling of plastic waste through a point-based system, allowing customers to access affordable food while contributing to a more environmentally friendly and sustainable ecosystem.

Methodology

Applying the Scrum methodology to the mobile application development process involves the formation of a team, appointment of a project master, determination of process schedules, selection of a product owner, creation of a product backlog, initiation of sprints, and closure of sprints. This project is a collaboration among developers within the Google Developer Student Club at IPB University. Our team consists of 4 members, and the team formation is divided into 3 roles: Hacker (Front-end developer & back-end developer), Hipster (UI & UX designer), and Hustler (project manager). I serve as a mobile front-end developer (the Hacker).

Architecture Code

The project adopts a modular code architecture within the Flutter Dart programming framework, featuring a well-organized folder structure. It includes dedicated folders such as Services, responsible for handling API calls and control; Pages, housing UI components; Utils, serving as a centralized hub for managing global variables and session management; Themes, responsible for handling design components.
