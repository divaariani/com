Problem

Pond and aquaculture management often involves manual, repetitive, and time-consuming tasks. Farmers and pond owners face several challenges such as monitoring water conditions, managing pump operations, and maintaining filtration systemsâ€”typically without automation or remote control. Additionally, financial tracking and business planning remain fragmented, with no centralized system for managing income, expenses, debts, or cultivation costs. This lack of integrated tools results in increased operational costs, potential harm to aquatic life, and inefficiencies in overall management.

Solution

This app designed to simplify and automate aquaculture and pond management for farmers, pond owners, and aquaculture professionals. Banoo leverages IoT integration to allow users to remotely monitor and control pond devices, including checking connectivity and toggling equipment on or off from their smartphones. Beyond device control, Banoo also addresses the business side of aquaculture with built-in financial management tools, enabling users to record and track income, expenses, debts, receivables, and loans. A cultivation calculator helps estimate costs and profits, aiding in business planning and decision-making. Built using the Flutter Dart framework for cross-platform functionality, Banoo combines smart automation with practical tools to enhance operational efficiency, reduce manual labor, and improve profitability for its users.

Methodology

The Waterfall methodology applied in the development of the application to ensure smooth analysis, design, implementation, testing, and distribution processes, resulting in an application that enables employees to easily attendance record, enhancing operational flexibility, and efficiency.

Architecture Code

The project adopts a MVC architecture within the Flutter Dart programming framework, featuring a well-organized folder structure. It includes dedicated folders such as Controllers, responsible for handling API calls and control; Pages, housing and viewing UI components; Models, responsible for handling datas; Utils, serving as a centralized hub for managing global variables and session management; Themes, responsible for handling design components.
